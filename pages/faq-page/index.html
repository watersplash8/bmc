I don’t have a summer job – will this mean that I get more OSAP?

OSAP assumes that you will work full-time over the summer months. They will automatically add in an expected pre-study income contribution from this period.

What are the benefits of joining VATC training vs VITA course?

VATC DISADVANTAGES: The list price for a VMware Authorized Training Center delivered Install, Configure, Manage (ICM) course is around $3000. It certainly is more than what BM College charge as an IT Academy partner to deliver the same course the same course with the same content.

The current Install, Configure, Manage (ICM) course delivered by VMware VATCs is 5 days in length, it’s very similar to a boot camp in a sense that you will be running through the content in order to get it all covered in such a short period of time.

The information is “blasted” for most that attend the course. This can be beneficial for attendees who already have at exposure or experience with vSphere, but the ones that are totally new won’t get much time to catch up and attention they need, because the instructor needs to quickly move through the content and deliver the course within four days and approximately one day of practical labs.

There is a lot more cost to a 5 day course than the $3k. If you work, it is costing somebody the capital that would have been earned by you working. Whether that is you doing it on your own or using PTO, or your employer covering your time. And for many people, it can mean travelling, which has its own costs and means that you can be away from family and other things (which could be considered good or bad, depending on who you are).

VATC BENEFIT: The major advantage of a commercial course is that you could get on a VMware VATC class almost immediately. For example you can sign up for the ICM class next week and qualify for VCP by the end of the week after the next week.

Speed of delivery is definitely the major advantage here. If your time is extremely valuable and you need to get certified immediately then, spending $3k for 5 day class might be your best option. Another advantage is the VATC delivered courses can be attended in your City in class setting.

What are the course prerequisites?

So how much exposure should someone have before taking the standard, 5-day ICM course to be able to handle it? Or in other words, how much of the material should you already know to avoid feeling like the instructor is a fire-hose of information? 25%? 50%? Well imagine even if you had to know ¼ of the material before taking a $3000 course, you would not be happy spending your own money on it. If you were totally new to vSphere, we feel it would be difficult to retain enough of the information with the intense schedule of the standard VATC course to justify the cost. Even with hand-holding, we am certain you would end up having to go over most of the material again from scratch on you own, in which case you might as well have just gone the self-study route to begin with. By comparison, the pace of our ICM course is more suitable to students new to vSphere. Some people prefer attending the standard course over the VITA course or self-study (especially if they don’t have to pay for it themselves and get time off work to attend), and/or will benefit enough from taking the standard course to justify the higher cost. We believe that the standard VATC ICM training has a place, as does our Academy model, and we fully appreciate that students have the choice between the two. Realistically speaking, it’s a lot of material to cover in 5 days. 40 hours in 5 days is an intense schedule, at least compared to the 40 hours over 4 weeks (or even 5 weeks) for the VITA ICM course.

Does BM College offer the online version of the course for students residing in Alaska, Arkansas, Idaho, Indiana, Kentucky, Maryland, Minnesota, Missouri, New Mexico, Oklahoma, and Wyoming?

Yes we do serve students in all of the US states.

I noticed it says 5.5/6.0 on the site, Will I be disqualified to take the 6.0 exam if i take the 5.5 course?

This course would satisfy the course requirement. After the course you can take either the VCP-550 or the VCP-600 exam, it’s your choice.

Can I join a class that is already in progress?

At BM College we cannot allow students in a class once it has started, but here is some information that may help you. We enroll students in the VMware courses in the order in which they registered on our online wait list. If you have not added your information to this list please do so here.

We apologize for any delay, but as you can imagine these classes are extremely popular. We make every effort to accommodate all students in a timely manner.

Can I sign up for the class right away or do I need to wait?

This is a continuing education class and no actual application is needed. You can sign up immediately, the actual sessions though are scheduled and you need to consume the content according to the restriction of up to 12 hours of training or lab time per week. You could do the labs and cover the material as quickly as you like… but you still have specific dates for the mid-term and final, plus you have to wait for the semester to officially be over in order to get credit for the course.

When should I expect to get logins/username/virtual class addresses?

After you sign up and pay for the course you will receive your account details almost immediately (usually within the next 10-20 minutes after payment is ocnfirmed). With that account you should be able to access the virtual classroom within the same day of the purchase. Access to the VMware Lab is provided on the first day of your official class start date. In other words you won’t have username/password to access the practice lab until the class start date.

Important: You need a valid email address for us to send me the login info. Please make sure to sign up with valid email address.

How do I prove to VMware that I have done the course?

At the beginning of the course each student is entered and attendance is tracked in VMware’s MyLearn database. Once the course is over the instructor will make sure to mark your attendance in VMware’s MyLearn database which is linked to PerasonVUE so you don’t have to prove to VMware the you were part of the ICM course. When you create a VMware account that account will be linked to the profile create for you by the instructor.
